What are Dental Implants

A dental implant is a metal post that replaces the root portion of a missing tooth. An artificial tooth (crown) is placed on an extension of the post (abutment) on the dental implant, giving you the look of a real tooth.

  • What is an All-on-4?
  • Who can benefit from All-on-4's?

The All-On-4 treatment is a revolutionary procedure used for patients with very few teeth or no teeth at all.

4 implants are inserted into the bone to support a full set of permanent, natural looking teeth.

If you have lost all - or most - of your original adult teeth,-or are suffering from the results of advanced periodontal (gum) disease, you are can benefit from All-on-4’s significantly.

You’re also likely to benefit if have existing loose or failing bridgework or wear partial dentures.

What is the Swiss 5 day System concept?


Our Swiss 5 Day Implants, developed by IHDE in Gommiswald, can be fitted in less time, meaning you’ll be fine to return home from your vacation within days together with a new beautiful smile.

  • Advancements
  • What is the Swiss 5 day system?
  • How it works?

Getting dental implants used to be a long process. 

But today techniques have advanced significantly.

Materials, healing times and procedure lengths have improved massively.

Normal Implants are inserted into the layer of spongy bone in the jaw – requiring 3 to 6 months for the implants to fuse with the bone (osseointegration) so that a permanent fixed set of teeth can be placed.

The revolutionary new implant procedure from Swiss company IHDE dental uses longer implants that are inserted into the deeper, hard layer of bone in the jaw. 

This provides an immediate foundation for a set of fixed teeth – and so eliminates the need for a return trip. Patients can leave with a beautiful smile in just 5 days. 

Day 1

Any old teeth are carefully extracted and the new implants fixed in place 

Day 2-4

You rest and take it easy while discussing your new teeth with your dentist

Day 5

Your customised new teeth are inserted onto your implants - and your new smile is complete
